🎉 Workplace Quiz Generator

Can I turn any topic into something people actually play?

Name a topic — famous Australians, 90s music, your team's in-jokes, your own family — and get back one self-contained HTML file: a player lobby, clue-by-clue reveals, a live scoreboard, a winner screen. No app, no login, no accounts. Open it on a phone, put the phone in the middle of the table, and let people shout.

The engine is a difficulty ladder: six clues per answer, hardest first, and guessing early is worth more than guessing safe. That's the rule that makes a room lean in rather than politely take turns — and the skill holds the line on it, so no clue gives the answer away at number three.

It will not save you a minute. That's the point: a build with no stakes rehearses the exact loop behind the serious ones — write the spec, iterate, judge the quality, ship it — with the pressure removed and the reps still counting.

Questions

Common questions

How is this different from Think With AI?

Think With AI is a trilogy of skills that help you make a decision — they turn a chat into a thinking partner that pushes back. Build With AI is the companion series: these ones hand you a product. Same licence, same format, different job. Decide over there, make it here.

Will this work on my work computer?

That's what it's built for. Every skill ships with a copy-paste kit that runs in any AI, including the Copilot your employer already provides. That isn't a downgrade — the quiz generator was first built exactly that way, inside a corporate fence, on a lunch break.

Are these free?

Yes. Free to use, adapt, and share under a Creative Commons Attribution licence. The only ask is that you keep the attribution to the original author. Whatever you build with them is yours — the licence covers the skills, not your output.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. Nothing here needs a developer setup, a terminal, or an install. You describe what you want in plain language and the AI writes the file; your job is judgement — spotting the clue that gives it away, the fact that's wrong, the screen that's awkward on a phone. These skills were all made by a non-developer.
