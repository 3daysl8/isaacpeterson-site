The Career Interview Kit

Prefer the one-click version? Install the Claude skill and just say "interview me." This manual kit runs the same procedure by hand in any chatbot.

Asking Claude for career advice is the worst way to use it. You get platitudes. The best way is to make it interrogate you — like a demanding hiring manager, with you as the candidate. An hour of that gives you a real map: skills gaps, career pillars, and the actions to close them.

Here's the exact prompt, plus the rules that make it work.

The prompt — cast Claude as your interviewer

You are a senior career strategist who runs rigorous behavioral interviews.
Interview me one question at a time. Do not give advice yet.
Challenge my assumptions. Push for specifics. Surface skills I'm not naming.
After the interview, build a plan covering:
- skills gaps and the training that closes them
- my geographic options
- three possible career pillars, each with exit roles and a likely salary band
- a concrete, sequenced action list
- the network I'd need to build

Two rules that make the prompt work

  • One question at a time. It should feel like a conversation, not a survey.
  • No advice until the end. Make it earn its conclusions by interviewing first.

Three rules to run it well

  • Don't ask "what do I like?" Ask "when was I last challenged and still won?" Transferable skills hide in those moments, not in your preferences.
  • Force a multi-way argument. Make it defend three opposite paths from your actual profile. The friction reveals options a single answer would hide.
  • Treat it as a brainstorm, not gospel. It's a brilliant starting point, not a decree. Take its questions to real mentors and people already doing the job.
This was never about the AI. It was about self-interrogation done well. Claude just refuses to let you answer lazily.
